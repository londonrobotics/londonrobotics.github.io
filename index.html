About

Welcome to the third edition of the London Robotics & AI Summer School.
This summer school will be in-person at King's College London on 25th June 2025.

Join us at King's College London for the third series of the London Summer School in Robotics & AI, supported by IEEE CIS and IET, jointly organised by King's College London (KCL), University College London (UCL), and Imperial College London (ICL). This third edition will be a, one-day event, with presentations, followed by a short robotic hackathon in which you will be challenged to make a robot tactile sensor and code a small robotic manipulation platform equipped with the tactile sensor.

Edward Johns

Imperial College London (ICL), UK

Title: Four Flavours of Data Generation in Robot Learning

Bio: Edward Johns is the Director of the Robot Learning Lab at Imperial College London, where he is also a Reader and a Royal Academy of Engineering (RAEng) Research Fellow. He received a BA and MEng in Electrical and Information Engineering from Cambridge University, and a PhD in vision-based robot localisation from Imperial College, supervised by Guang-Zhong Yang in the Hamlyn Centre. Following his PhD, he spent a year as a postdoc at UCL working with Gabriel Brostow, and he then returned to Imperial College as a founding member of the Dyson Robotics Lab with Andrew Davison, where he led the robot manipulation team. In 2017, he was awarded an RAEng Research Fellowship, and then in 2018 he was appointed as a Lecturer at Imperial College, and founded the Robot Learning Lab. Alongside leading the lab's research, he teaches a graduate-level course on Robot Learning.
Lorenzo Jamone
University College London (UCL), UK

Lorenzo Jamone

University College London (UCL), UK

Title: The Intelligence of the Tactile Hand

Bio: Lorenzo Jamone is an Associate Professor in Robotics and Director of the CRISP group at the Department of Computer Science of University College London (UCL). He is also a Senior Lecturer in Robotics at the School of Engineering and Materials Science (SEMS) of the Queen Mary University of London (QMUL) and associated to ARQ (Advanced Robotics at Queen Mary). Since October 2018, he has also been a Turing Fellow at The Alan Turing Institute. His main research interest is in cognitive Robotics: building intelligent robots by taking inspiration from humans, and validating theories of human cognition by testing computational models on robots. Within this general approach, his research focuses on "The Intelligence of the Hand", in robots and humans. This includes: visuo-haptic perception and exploration of objects, grasping, dexterous manipulation, affordance perception, tool use, body schema, eye-hand coordination, physical interaction and collaboration, non-verbal communication, the sense of touch. He also collaborates with VisLab, at the Instituto de Sistemas e Robotica of the Instituto Superior Tecnico (Lisbon, Portugal), as a visiting associate researcher in humanoid and cognitive robotics, and with SuganoLab, at the Department of Modern Mechanical Engineering of Waseda University (Tokyo, Japan), as a visiting professor in tactile sensing for robotic manipulation. Before, he was with the Biped Group of Takanishi Lab (Waseda University), and with the RBCS (Robotics Brain and Cognitive Science) Department of the IIT (Italian Institute of Technology).
